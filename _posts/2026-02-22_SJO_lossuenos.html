Road Work, Closures & Active Alerts

🚨

CRITICAL — Tárcoles Bridge Single-Lane Construction

Location: Tárcoles River Bridge on Route 34 (Costanera Sur) — mandatory crossing between Route 27 and Herradura.

Effective: Monday, February 23, 2026 through March 27, 2026 (initial phase). Resumes May 4–9, 2026.

Condition: Alternating single-lane traffic managed by on-site traffic police. One direction moves at a time; the other direction waits. Speed limit reduced to 30 km/h on the bridge structure.

Expected delays: 30 minutes to over 1 hour, particularly in late mornings and afternoons, weekends, and holidays. Build-up can be significant in peak dry season (current period).

Night closures: Both lanes may close entirely between 9:00 PM and 5:00 AM on some nights in mid-March through mid-April. If traveling late, verify bridge status beforehand.

Project context: This is a $6.7 million structural rehabilitation funded by the Central American Bank for Economic Integration. The project is currently 61% complete and essential for the long-term safety of this critical coastal corridor. Sources: Tico Times • Q Costa Rica • Travel & Tour World

⏰

ONGOING — Route 27 Sunday Lane Reversal Program

Applies every Sunday: All outbound lanes (toward Pacific coast) on Route 27 are reversed to flow toward San José from approximately 1:00 PM to 7:00 PM.

Why it exists: On Sunday afternoons, tens of thousands of Costa Rican families return from beach weekends. MOPT (the Ministry of Public Works and Transport) reverses lanes to ease this enormous inbound flow.

Impact for you: Outbound travel from the airport toward Los Sueños is not possible on Route 27 during this window. If arriving on a Sunday, plan to be in your car and on the road before noon, or wait until after 7:00 PM (but see night driving caution). Sources: Tico Times (Feb 17, 2026) • Tico Times (Jan 3, 2026)

ℹ

MONITORING — Route 27 Long-Term Widening Project

Status: Costa Rica's National Development Plan (2023–2026) includes a major widening of Route 27 from San José to the Pacific coast. Phase 1 focuses on the segment near San José (from the National Gymnasium to Siquiares). A new overpass is planned as part of this phase.

Impact for February 2026: No confirmed active lane closures attributable to this project were found for your travel dates. The project is still in contract renegotiation with the highway concessionaire Globalvia. Monitor Waze for any newly announced work zones.

Weather & Your Drive

  • ☀
    Road conditions are excellent in February. Rain-slicked roads, flash flooding, and mudslides — significant concerns during the May–October rainy season — are essentially non-factors in February. Route 27 and Route 34 will be dry and in good condition.
  • 🌞
    Sun glare during the afternoon drive. Traveling westward toward the coast in the afternoon means driving into the sun. A good pair of polarized sunglasses is recommended, and your vehicle's sun visors will be useful.
  • ⚡
    Tropical weather can surprise. While significant rain in February is very unlikely, brief afternoon showers are not impossible. The tropics are rarely 100% predictable. The probability is low, but if a shower occurs, visibility can drop quickly — reduce your speed and turn on headlights.

Route 27 is a privately operated toll highway (concession held by Globalvia). You will pass through four main toll plazas, plus a possible exit ramp toll. Come prepared with either a credit card or small-denomination bills.

Safety, Driving Tips & Essential Know-How

  • 📹
    Use Waze, not Google Maps. Waze is the dominant navigation app in Costa Rica and is actively maintained by a large local user base. It provides real-time bridge alerts, traffic slowdowns, police locations, and road hazards. Download it before you land and search for "Los Sueños Resort and Marina." Keep an offline map downloaded as a backup.
  • 📄
    Documents to carry at all times. Keep your original US passport (not a photocopy), your original driver's license, your rental car contract, and your insurance documents accessible in the vehicle. Costa Rican traffic police (Tránsitos) conduct routine stops and are professional and courteous. A valid US driver's license is fully sufficient — no international permit is required.
  • 🌞
    Arrive before dark if at all possible. Costa Rica's roads outside the San José metro area often lack reflective lane markings and adequate lighting. Wildlife — including large iguanas, sloths, and dogs — can appear on the road without warning. Aim to reach Los Sueños by 6:00 PM. If you cannot avoid traveling after dark, reduce your speed, stay alert, and use your high beams where safe to do so.
  • 🚗
    Drive defensively, not aggressively. Costa Rican drivers frequently change lanes without signaling, make abrupt merges, and pass on highways at high speed. Road rage is not the norm — this is simply the local driving culture. Maintain safe following distances, anticipate unpredictable lane changes, and do not respond to aggressive driving behavior.
  • 🚫
    Do not stop on isolated stretches of road. Near SJO, there have been documented incidents where vehicles are deliberately struck with stones or debris to force a roadside stop, where the occupants are then robbed. If your vehicle is struck or you hear an unusual noise in an isolated area, drive to the nearest gas station, hotel, or populated area before stopping and inspecting. Route 27 itself is generally safe, but this caution applies to secondary roads.
  • 📌
    Speed limits are in km/h, not mph. On Route 27 the limit is typically 80–100 km/h (50–62 mph). In towns: 40–60 km/h (25–37 mph). Speed cameras and police with radar guns are active on Route 27. Fines are substantial and are assessed against the rental vehicle if the driver cannot be identified. Drive at or slightly below the posted limit.
  • ✅
    Rental car insurance — verify before you go. Costa Rica requires mandatory third-party liability (TPL) insurance, which most rental companies include. However, many US credit cards that offer rental car coverage do not cover the Costa Rican mandatory liability component. Verify with your credit card company before declining any rental insurance. A zero-deductible CDW (collision damage waiver) is strongly advisable given local road conditions.
  • ⛽
    Fuel up before leaving the San José area. Gas stations become less frequent as you head toward the coast. There are stations in Orotina (near the end of Route 27) and near Jacó, but do not assume availability. Fill your tank at one of the many gas stations near the airport or along Route 27 early in your journey. Gas stations in Costa Rica are full service — attendants pump the gas. Confirm the gauge resets to zero before they begin, and verify your total before paying.
  • 🏈
    Speed bumps ("topes") appear without warning. Unmarked or poorly marked speed bumps are common on roads in Costa Rica, including near toll plazas and approaching towns. Reduce speed when approaching any populated area and watch for the road surface changing.

About Los Sueños Resort & Marina

Los Sueños Resort & Marina is consistently regarded as one of the finest resort destinations in Latin America — a 1,100-acre private enclave set on the sheltered waters of Herradura Bay, Costa Rica's Central Pacific coast. Here is what to expect upon arrival.

  • 🏹
    Sport Fishing — You Have Arrived at the Right Time. Los Sueños is widely known as the "Billfish Capital of the World." Deep water rises close to shore, and February falls squarely within peak sailfish season (December–April). Marlin, mahi-mahi, tuna, wahoo, and roosterfish are all active. Full-day and half-day charters depart from the marina daily.
  • ⛳
    La Iguana Championship Golf Course. An 18-hole course designed by Ted Robinson, set within tropical rainforest with ocean views. Considered among Costa Rica's premier golf experiences.
  • 🍕
    Dining options on-site. The Marina Village hosts approximately 10 restaurants and bars, ranging from casual dockside dining to the Lanterna Italian Steakhouse, Bambú sushi, and Al Fresco beach club fare. No need to leave the property for excellent meals.
  • 🌳
    Nearby: Carara National Park. Just 15 minutes north of the resort along Route 34, Carara is a world-renowned birding destination and the best place in Costa Rica to observe scarlet macaws in the wild — a truly unforgettable experience, especially at dawn or dusk when the flocks fly over.
